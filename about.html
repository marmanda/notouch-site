A different way of making music

No-touch explores how sound can emerge from movement without relying on keys, strings, or surfaces. I design interfaces that respond to distance, direction, and the quality of motion, turning space itself into a musical environment. The goal is simple: instruments that are minimal, readable, and deeply expressive — tools that invite exploration rather than demanding technique.

Why gesture

Gesture is immediate. It carries intention, energy, and nuance long before it becomes sound. In notouch, gesture is not a command but a continuous field of possibilities. Movement shapes pitch, harmony, timbre, and intensity through smooth transitions, without thresholds or discrete steps. This approach creates instruments that feel alive: responsive, fluid, and open to interpretation.

Design principles

I follow a set of principles that guide every instrument: Simplicity — reduce visual and cognitive load Legibility — make the behavior of the instrument immediately understandable Continuity — avoid jumps, thresholds, or binary states Expressivity — allow fine control without requiring virtuosity Accessibility — design for diverse bodies and abilities These principles help create instruments that are both simple and rich, minimal and expressive.

Handwave Synth

It is a flexible pitch system that moves seamlessly between tonal, semi‑tonal, and free atonal behavior. Two controls shape this continuum: 1) Tonal Strength defines how strongly the pitch is pulled toward scale tones. 2) Scale Selection chooses the harmonic framework. With Tonal Strength at 0%, pitch is fully continuous. At 100%, it snaps precisely to the chosen scale. Intermediate values create expressive glides that gently settle into stable notes. Handwave Synth includes a curated set of scales — Diatonic, Pentatonic, and Hijaz — allowing the performer to move from structured melodic playing to open, gesture‑driven sound.
