Collection of no-touch instruments

This collection brings together a growing family of no-touch exploratory tools designed to spark curiosity, wonder, creativity, and wellbeing. Each tool invites children to engage with materials, sounds, movement, and sensory experiences through open-ended exploration. Rather than focusing on a predefined outcome, these experiences encourage observation, experimentation, self-expression, and self-regulation. To help navigate the collection, the tools are organized into seven categories that reflect the primary type of experience they offer:

  • Material Exploration
  • Discovery & Exploration
  • Wonder & Curiosity
  • Sound Exploration
  • Movement & Transformation
  • Creation & Composition
  • Wellbeing & Self-Regulation
While each tool belongs to a main category, many naturally connect to multiple areas, creating rich opportunities for play, learning, and personal growth.

Harmony Pads

It is a gesture‑controlled musical interface built around three adaptive oval zones. Both hands can interact with the system, but only one oval can be active at a time, ensuring a clear and intentional musical output. Each oval corresponds to a musical chord: tonic in the middle, subdominant in the left, and dominant in the right. The dynamic intensity of the sound is controlled by the hand’s proximity to the center of the active oval. As the performer moves closer to the centre, the system responds visually and sonically: the border thickness increases, creating a clear visual cue and the sound intensity rises, following a smooth, continuous curve. Harmony Pads offers a minimal, expressive environment for shaping harmonic textures through simple, embodied gestures.
