No‑touch instruments are digital musical interfaces that allow users to play, shape and explore sound without any physical contact. They rely on hand‑tracking, computer vision and gesture‑based interaction to translate movement into expressive musical actions.

These instruments are designed to be accessible, intuitive and inclusive, enabling meaningful musical experiences for everyone — including individuals with motor or neurological disabilities, special educational needs and diverse learning profiles.

Harmony Pads

Is a gesture‑controlled musical interface built around three adaptive oval zones. Both hands can interact with the system, but only one oval can be active at a time, ensuring a clear and intentional musical output. Ecah oval corresponds to a musical chord: tonic ind the middle, subdominant in the left, and dominant in the right. The dynamic intensity of the sound is controlled by the hand’s proximity to the center of the active oval. As the performer moves closer tp the centre, the system responds visually and sonically: the border thickness increases, creating a clear visual cue and the sound intensity rises, following a smooth, continuous curve. Harmony Pads offers a minimal, expressive environment for shaping harmonic textures through simple, embodied gestures.
