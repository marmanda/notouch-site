No‑touch instruments are digital musical interfaces that allow users to play, shape and explore sound without any physical contact. They rely on hand‑tracking, computer vision and gesture‑based interaction to translate movement into expressive musical actions.

These instruments are designed to be accessible, intuitive and inclusive, enabling meaningful musical experiences for everyone — including individuals with motor or neurological disabilities, special educational needs and diverse learning profiles.

Harmony Pads

It is a gesture‑controlled musical interface built around three adaptive oval zones. Both hands can interact with the system, but only one oval can be active at a time, ensuring a clear and intentional musical output. Each oval corresponds to a musical chord: tonic in the middle, subdominant in the left, and dominant in the right. The dynamic intensity of the sound is controlled by the hand’s proximity to the center of the active oval. As the performer moves closer to the centre, the system responds visually and sonically: the border thickness increases, creating a clear visual cue and the sound intensity rises, following a smooth, continuous curve. Harmony Pads offers a minimal, expressive environment for shaping harmonic textures through simple, embodied gestures.

Fingernote

It is a no‑touch, pinch‑based melodic instrument built around two independent pitch zones: lower zone C3–B3, higher zone C4–C5. Each hand is monophonic, always selecting the finger with the smallest finger‑to‑thumb distance. When that distance falls below the activation threshold, the corresponding note is triggered. The dynamic intensity of the sound is controlled by the distance between the active finger and the screen. As the finger moves closer to the screen, the sound becomes stronger, and as it moves away, the sound becomes softer. Fingernote creates a compact, expressive environment where musical phrasing emerges from micro‑gestures, pinch transitions, and depth‑based dynamics.

Handwave Synth

Is a flexible pitch system that moves seamlessly between tonal, semi‑tonal, and free atonal behavior. Two controls shape this continuum: 1) Tonal Strength defines how strongly the pitch is pulled toward scale tones 2) Scale Selection chooses the harmonic framework With Tonal Strength at 0%, pitch is fully continuous. At 100%, it snaps precisely to the chosen scale. Intermediate values create expressive glides that gently settle into stable notes. Handwave Synth includes a curated set of scales — Diatonic, Pentatonic, and Hijaz — allowing the performer to move from structured melodic playing to open, gesture‑driven sound.
